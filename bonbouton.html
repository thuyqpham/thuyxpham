Commercializing graphene based smart clothes

Chief Designer

Bonbouton is startup which is based on co-founder's patent for thermal sensing graphene based ink.  When I joined the startup it had no product when I joined as Chief Designer.  My role was to come up with products, namely integration into textiles for the usage of  "smart clothes".  The "smart clothes" industry was in its infancy at the time and commercialization of such product would have been considered groundbreaking.  However, it is extremely challenging because of manufacturing chain of electronics and apparel are quite different in many respects.  Solution is required to adapt existing manufacturing processes as well as adhere to limitations of graphene ink printing technology. During my tenure I came up with sensor on ribbon as a textile impletation for apparel and thermal sensing sandal for diabetic ulcer early detection. Seed investment was successfully raised for the sandal product.

adapting existing manufacturing processes

Graphene printing.  Printing the graphene is done on a specialized ink jet printer.  The curing process requires extreme high heat so the substrate used is Kapton, which is common for flexible electronics.

Slitting.  After the ink is printed on Kapton substrate, the film is sent to a factory that uses a mechanical slitting process to cut down rolls of film into 0.6mm "slits".

Ribbon weaving.  Together with copper twisted thread and regular textile, the slits are woven into a ribbon.  Copper is woven on the warp and graphene is woven on the weft spaced 1m apart.  The reason for this is to conduct electrical current across the graphene in order to measure resistance.  Resistance is correlated with temperature.

USB connection.  It is important to use copper because we need a connection to CPU to process data from sensor.  Other smart clothes system opt for silver coated thread for conductivity & proprietary connector to CPU usually metal snaps.  Our system is superior bc  superior signal to noise ratio of copper and USB allow us to make smaller HW component of system

CPU/ hardware component.  This component reads data from sensor and sends to app via Bluetooth.  It also contains other sensors like accelerometer, etc.. for FitBit like functionality.

App.  Native app IOS & Android controls system and data visualization for user

parts of the ecosystem
  • Textile integration of sensor
  • Interface to electronics
  • Hardware component/ CPU/ Bluetooth
  • Mobile app for control and data visualization
