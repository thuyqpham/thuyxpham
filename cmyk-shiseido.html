Appliance for dispensing nail polish

Design thinking & UX design

CMYK is a case study pitch to cosmetics company Shiseido as a side project of former Bonbouton engineers.  The idea is an appliance that can dispense nail polish of any color using CMYK color theory common to printing.  It is meant for professionals in a salon setting to replace hundreds of vials of nail polish.

how it works
  • Mobile to analyze color from camera picture
  • Appliance to mix and dispense nail polish
  • Chemical formulation of paint
case study

Graphene printing.  Printing the graphene is done on a specialized ink jet printer.  The curing process requires extreme high heat so the substrate used is Kapton, which is common for flexible electronics.

Slitting.  After the ink is printed on Kapton substrate, the film is sent to a factory that uses a mechanical slitting process to cut down rolls of film into 0.6mm "slits".

Design solution

Sensor on ribbon with USB interface for HW component can be shipped to apparel makers to as sewn-in solution

Sensor on ribbon

Using slitting and weaving we could integrate graphene printed Kapton film into textile.

Hardware component

USB connection allows us to design small HW.

Mobile app

Data is sent via Bluetooth to native app.
